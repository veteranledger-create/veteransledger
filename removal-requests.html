Removal Requests

VeteranLedger Historical Archive

1. Purpose

This page provides a process for copyright holders, rights owners, or any concerned party to request the removal or modification of content displayed in the VeteranLedger Archive. We take intellectual property rights and ethical concerns seriously.

2. Grounds for Removal

Removal requests will be considered on the following grounds:

  • Copyright infringement — content used without proper license or authorization
  • License violation — Creative Commons or other license terms not properly followed
  • Privacy concerns — content that violates privacy rights of individuals
  • Defamatory or harmful content — inaccurate or damaging information about identifiable individuals
  • Ethical concerns — content that is exploitative, dehumanizing, or presented without proper context
  • Right of publicity — unauthorized use of a person's likeness

3. Request Process

To submit a removal request, please provide the following information:

  1. Identification — your name, affiliation (if any), and contact information
  2. Content identification — specific URL(s) of the content in question
  3. Description — what the content is and why removal is requested
  4. Legal basis — the specific grounds for removal (copyright, privacy, etc.)
  5. Rights verification — evidence that you are the rights holder or authorized representative
  6. Declaration — a statement that the information provided is accurate and complete

4. Contact Information

Please send removal requests to the following contact points:

Email: removals [at] veteranledger [dot] org

Subject line: Removal Request: [Content Identifier]

Response time: We will acknowledge receipt within 5 business days and respond substantively within 15 business days.

5. What to Expect

  • Receipt of your request will be acknowledged within 5 business days
  • Each request will be reviewed on its merits
  • We may request additional information to process your request
  • If the request is valid, content will be removed or modified promptly
  • If the request is declined, we will provide reasoning and alternative options
  • All requests are handled confidentially and logged for record-keeping

6. Counter-Notifications

If you believe that content was removed in error, you may submit a counter-notification containing:

  • Identification of the removed content and its original location
  • Your contact information
  • Statement explaining why removal was in error
  • Consent to jurisdiction for dispute resolution

7. Good-Faith Requests

We are committed to resolving concerns amicably and in good faith. Before submitting a formal legal notice, we encourage you to contact us to discuss your concerns. Many issues can be resolved through dialogue without formal proceedings.

8. Record of Actions

A record of removal actions and their justifications is maintained for archival transparency. Individual requesters' personal information is not published.
