LETTERS OF AXIS SOLDIERS

FIELD POST · PERSONAL CORRESPONDENCE · 1939–1945

World War II changed the lives of millions of people across the world. This personal archive explores letters written by soldiers during the war — messages sent from the front lines filled with fear, hope, and longing for home. The project aims to present a human perspective of the Second World War beyond battles and military statistics.

HISTORICAL AND EDUCATIONAL PURPOSES ONLY

This page is presented for historical and educational purposes only. It does not endorse or promote any ideology. All materials are documented primary sources from official archives, presented with respect for the human dignity of those involved. The content focuses on factual documentation without demonization or inflammatory rhetoric. Every letter has been legally verified for public domain status or appropriate licensing.
