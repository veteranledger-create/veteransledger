Transparency Policy

VeteranLedger Historical Archive

1. Editorial Independence

VeteranLedger is an independent historical documentary project. It is not affiliated with any government, political organization, military institution, or ideological group. Editorial decisions are made independently, guided solely by historical accuracy, documentary integrity, and educational purpose.

2. Sourcing Methodology

Content in this Archive is assembled from:

  • Primary sources — official military records, contemporary documents, photographs, and archival materials
  • Secondary sources — peer-reviewed historical scholarship, academic monographs, and verified historical databases
  • Archival repositories — Bundesarchiv, Wikimedia Commons, national archives, and institutional collections

All sources are attributed where possible. When direct attribution cannot be provided due to the nature of the source material, this is noted in the entry.

3. AI-Generated Imagery Disclosure

This Archive may include AI-assisted reconstructions where original archival imagery is unavailable, restricted, or does not exist. The following principles govern the use of AI-generated content:

When AI Imagery Is Used

  • No public-domain alternative exists — the historical subject is not represented in known archival collections
  • Licensing restrictions prevent lawful reuse — existing imagery is under copyright and rights-holders cannot be identified or contacted
  • Redistribution rights were not granted — archival material exists but is not licensed for educational redistribution
  • Historical material is incomplete or missing — records were destroyed, lost, or never created
  • Diagrammatic or technical clarity — complex equipment or formations benefit from clean schematic representations

How AI Imagery Is Identified

AI-generated reconstructions are clearly labeled with an AI Reconstruction badge and include a disclosure in the image attribution noting the nature of the image. The caption context will also indicate when a visual is a reconstruction rather than an original archival photograph.

Preference for Authentic Imagery

Authentic archival imagery is always preferred whenever available and lawfully usable. AI-generated content is a supplementary tool, not a replacement for genuine historical documentation. As the public-domain corpus expands, we will progressively replace AI-assisted images with authentic archival material.

Limitations of AI Reconstructions

AI-generated images are interpretive reconstructions, not historical documents. They may contain anachronisms, inaccuracies, or artistic interpretations that differ from historical reality. Users should treat AI-generated content as illustrative reference material, not as primary evidence.

4. Image Attribution Commitment

Every image in the Archive includes attribution that specifies:

  • Source — the repository, collection, or rights-holder
  • License — the specific Creative Commons, Public Domain, or other license governing use
  • Credit line — how the source requests to be credited
  • Nature — whether the image is an original archival photograph, an AI reconstruction, or a diagram

No attribution information is removed or simplified. Only the presentation format is optimized for readability.

5. Correction Policy

We are committed to accuracy. If you identify:

  • Factual errors in historical content
  • Incorrect or missing attributions
  • Problematic or offensive content
  • Copyright or licensing concerns

Please contact us through the Removal Requests page. All legitimate concerns will be investigated and addressed promptly.

6. Funding and Conflicts of Interest

This Archive is a non-commercial, unfunded educational project. No revenue is generated from its operation. No external organization has editorial influence over its content. No conflicts of interest exist between the project's educational mission and external entities.

7. Archival Integrity

We are committed to preserving the historical record without distortion, sensationalism, or political agenda. Content is presented with:

  • Neutral, factual language in documentary descriptions
  • Clear separation of historical facts from editorial commentary
  • Full attribution of sources to enable independent verification
  • Contextual framing that acknowledges the complexity of historical events
